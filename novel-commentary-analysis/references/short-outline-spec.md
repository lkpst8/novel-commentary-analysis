# Short Outline Specification

## Goal

Compress a long novel into a short-form but complete plot outline without inventing missing material or collapsing the back half into vagueness.

## Required Structure

The short outline should still cover:

1. opening situation
2. triggering event
3. main conflict escalation
4. midpoint or major reversal
5. late major turn
6. final decision or confrontation
7. ending and residue

## Compression Process

Always use the staged chain:

1. packet notes
2. phase notes
3. ledgers
4. `compression-pass-1-full.md`
5. `compression-pass-2-medium.md`
6. `compression-pass-3-short.md`
7. `short-outline-canon.md`

Do not jump from raw text straight to the short outline unless the source is genuinely small.

## Keep / Compress Rules

Keep:

- main-line causality
- story-direction turns
- ending-relevant characters
- ending-relevant subplots
- emotional residue of the ending

Compress:

- repeated local conflict cycles
- repetitive bridge beats
- atmosphere-only material not required for causal understanding
- minor characters who do not alter the main line

## Anti-Hallucination Rules

- Do not invent bridge scenes between preserved events.
- Do not fabricate motives that were only weakly implied.
- Do not write a cleaner ending than the original source supports.

## Anti-Omission Rules

- Do not let the later half shrink into a vague ending paragraph.
- Do not remove the event that explains why the ending becomes possible.
- Do not keep a subplot in the outline unless its effect on the whole story is visible.

## Multi-Part Output Rule

If the final short outline is too large for one model output:

- use `short-outline-canon.md` as the fixed source
- split the final output into ordered parts
- do not continue from memory alone
