# Long-Novel Commentary Framework

## Use This Reference

Use this file when the request needs more than a quick summary. It provides a reusable method for turning a long novel into a stable commentary structure without losing chronology, motive, or thematic depth.

## Core Deliverables

A solid long-novel commentary workflow usually produces some combination of:

- `剧情总纲`: the whole-book skeleton in chronological order
- `人物弧光表`: the state-change map for major characters
- `关系张力表`: who pulls whom, who blocks whom, and why it matters
- `主题句`: one to three claims about what the novel is ultimately expressing
- `解说稿`: a user-facing article, spoken script, or episode plan

## Five-Pass Method

### Pass 1: Establish the spine

Answer these without overexplaining:

- Where and when does the story live?
- Who is the emotional center?
- What problem locks the story in motion?
- What kind of ending does the novel seem to be moving toward?

Output:

- One-sentence premise
- One-paragraph story frame
- Early list of major characters

### Pass 2: Build the plot ladder

Split the novel into 5 to 12 dramatic stages. Prefer actual shifts in conflict over arbitrary chapter counts.

For each stage, note:

- Goal
- Obstacle
- Turn
- Consequence

Template:

```text
Stage X:
Goal:
Obstacle:
Turn:
Consequence:
```

### Pass 3: Build the character engine

For each major character, capture:

- Public identity
- Private wound or desire
- Defensive strategy
- Key relationship dependencies
- Best moment
- Worst moment
- Final state

Useful arc labels:

- `成长型`: becomes more self-aware or responsible
- `坍塌型`: loses structure, status, or moral center
- `困守型`: does not truly change and pays for it
- `反噬型`: harms others through the very trait that once protected them
- `献祭型`: gives up self-interest for loyalty, love, duty, or belief

### Pass 4: Extract meaning

Theme is not a slogan. Build it from repeated evidence:

- repeated settings or social spaces
- repeated emotional situations
- repeated power asymmetries
- repeated speech patterns
- repeated choices under pressure

Good theme questions:

- What kind of love, loyalty, or ambition does the novel distrust?
- Which institution shapes the characters most: family, class, army, workplace, city, generation?
- What illusion does the novel slowly dismantle?
- What pain keeps repeating across different characters?

### Pass 5: Turn analysis into performance

When converting notes into a解说稿, write with staged momentum:

1. Hook the contradiction
2. Define the world
3. Tell the spine
4. Pause at major turns and explain why they matter
5. Lift to character and theme
6. End with emotional residue, not just plot closure

## Output Templates

### Template A: Long article commentary

```text
标题/立意句
开场钩子
故事背景与核心冲突
第一阶段剧情
第二阶段剧情
中段升级与关系变化
后段崩塌/反转
结局与余韵
人物弧光总结
主题提炼
为什么这部小说值得讲
```

### Template B: Video or podcast narration

```text
开场问题
用一句话立住作品气质
按阶段复盘剧情
每个阶段补一句人物心理或关系解释
在三处关键节点做主题拔高
结尾给出整体判断与记忆点
```

### Template C: Serial episode plan

Use 6 to 12 episodes for one long novel unless the user asks otherwise.

For each episode:

- Episode title
- Coverage scope
- Core conflict
- Key reveal
- Emotional hook ending

## Common Failure Modes

- Retelling everything at equal weight. Fix by ranking turning points.
- Summarizing events without causality. Fix by forcing each section to answer “why next?”
- Praising or condemning characters too early. Fix by describing behavior before moral labeling.
- Treating theme as a slogan detached from scenes. Fix by tying each claim to a repeated pattern.
- Flattening secondary characters who act as mirrors, foils, or pressure points. Fix by recording each relational function.

## Fast Heuristics

- If readers mainly remember the people, lead with人物关系.
- If readers mainly remember the pain, lead with emotional structure.
- If readers mainly remember the era, lead with institution and class atmosphere.
- If the book has many incidents but one dominant contradiction, summarize plot faster and spend more space on pressure and choice.
- If the book is melodramatic, explain why the emotional excess works instead of treating it as a flaw by default.

## Packet-by-Packet Reading Loop

When working from packetized files:

1. Read one packet.
2. Extract events, new characters, reversals, and emotional changes.
3. Append notes to a rolling timeline.
4. Mark unresolved threads.
5. After all packets, merge repeated observations and remove contradictions.

Do not write the final theme after packet one unless the evidence is overwhelming. Let the full-book pattern emerge.
